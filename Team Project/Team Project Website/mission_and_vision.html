Structural health monitoring (SHM) in general sense is a process aimed at providing accurate and timely information about the condition and performance of a structure. A need for SHM arises with the fact that properties of both concrete and steel depend on a large number of factors which are often hard to predict in practice. SHM helps is to monitor the in-situ behaviour of a structure accurately and efficiently, to assess its performance under various service loads, to detect damage or deterioration, and to determine the health of the structure. By just a small adjustment to the construction, a safer life is possible.
